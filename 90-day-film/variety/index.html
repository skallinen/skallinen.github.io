Helsinki International Film Festival and DocPoint Launch '90 Day Film' to Bring International Directors and Producers to Helsinki — and Lay the Ground for a New Production Incentive

Finland's leading documentary and international film festivals are joining forces to commission nine international filmmakers to shoot in Helsinki, backed by city funding and a forthcoming Helsinki City production incentive that will reshape the Nordic production landscape.

By Variety Staff April 15, 2026
Note to reader: This is a speculative concept article, shared as part of a user research exercise. The 90 Day Film program and the Helsinki City production incentive described below do not yet exist.

Two of Finland's most prominent film institutions are teaming up on a new short film initiative that highlights long-term production attraction. DocPoint, Helsinki's international documentary film festival, and HIFF, the Helsinki International Film Festival, are set to jointly launch 90 Day Film — a program that will invite nine international filmmakers to spend up to 90 days in the Finnish capital, shoot a 90-second to 9 minute film using local locations and local crew, equipment and talent, and receive €90,000 in commission funding to do it. The program is supported by the City of Helsinki and is modeled on the 90 Day Finn initiative, the city's internationally recognized effort to attract global tech talent that has operated since 2021.

The festival pedigree matters here. By anchoring the program in DocPoint and HIFF — which hosts Finland's biggest film festival Love & Anarchy as well as the industry event Finnish Film Affair — rather than a municipal office, organizers are signaling that the target participant is a working filmmaker with a festival career, not a tourist or a brand ambassador. The selection process will prioritize directors, producers and showrunners with feature or series credits or significant international festival history who have not previously worked in Finland — people whose experience of Helsinki's production ecosystem carries credibility when they talk about it afterward.

"The 90 Day Film is proof of concept. What we're really commissioning is nine informed opinions about what it means to make work here."

— Program spokesperson

The structure is deliberately compact. Each selected filmmaker receives a fixed €90,000 production budget, access to a Helsinki-based talent, equipment and crew, locations, and a residency desk in the city center — echoing the concierge infrastructure that made 90 Day Finn a well-documented template for city-level talent attraction. The 90-second to 9 minute format is a constraint by design: short enough to complete within the residency window, long enough to constitute a genuine cinematic statement and a practical introduction to the local industry.

What elevates the program from a commission scheme to a strategic instrument is the incentive layer behind it. Business Helsinki is preparing to announce a production incentive for larger international productions — a cash rebate mechanism that would stack on top of the country's existing support infrastructure and, its architects argue, position Helsinki competitively against other European countries such as Ireland and the Czech Republic for mid-budget features and streaming series. When the two instruments are taken together — the 90 Day Film program as an experiential gateway, the Business Finland and Business Helsinki incentives as the financial case for returning with a larger project — organizers describe a package with no direct equivalent operating in Northern Europe.

90 Day Film — key terms

  • Organizers: DocPoint and HIFF, in cooperation with the City of Helsinki
  • 9 international filmmakers selected per annual cohort
  • €90,000 production commission per participant, all-in
  • Deliverable: one 90-second to 9 min film, shot on Helsinki locations with Finnish crew
  • Residency support: co-working space, local line producer, location access
  • Separate track: forthcoming Business Finland and Business Helsinki production incentive for features and series
  • Combined package positioned as the most competitive offering for international productions in Northern Europe

The timing is deliberate. The global production incentive landscape has been in flux, and for smaller European territories, the window to differentiate is narrowing. A credible, well-structured incentive announced in conjunction with a visible filmmaker program — rather than as a dry policy instrument — stands a better chance of cutting through the noise reaching production company slates for 2027 and beyond.

For DocPoint and HIFF, the program also represents an expansion of mandate. Both festivals have spent years building relationships with international directors through their programming; 90 Day Film gives them a mechanism to convert those relationships into production activity on Finnish soil. The finished films will screen as part of festival programming at Finnish Film Affair in September 2026, giving the cohort an immediate audience and giving the city a tangible cultural return on its investment.

Applications for the inaugural cohort will open in spring 2026, with the films being shown and celebrated at Finnish Film Affair in September 2026. Directors, producers and showrunners interested in the program can find full details, including eligibility criteria and the application timeline, at the official 90 Day Film site. The Business Helsinki production incentive is anticipated to be formally announced before the end of the calendar year.
