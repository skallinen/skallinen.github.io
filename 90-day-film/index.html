HIFF and DocPoint are two festivals that embody the best of Finnish values: being honest and kind, being bold and brave, and standing up for what's right. These are core values found throughout Finnish culture — the happiest folks in the world nine years running — and you'll find them in every industry connection you make and every crew you build.

About the Program

What is 90 Day Film?

90 Day Film is Helsinki's filmmaker residency program, jointly organised by DocPoint (Helsinki's international documentary film festival) and HIFF (Helsinki International Film Festival), in cooperation with the City of Helsinki.

Each year, nine international filmmakers — from established directors, producers and showrunners to bold emerging voices — are invited to spend 90 days in Helsinki. Each receives a €90,000 production commission, a Helsinki-based production crew, equipment, locations, talent, and temporary accommodation.

The format is deliberately compact: a 90-second to 9-minute film designed to be short enough to complete within the residency window, long enough to constitute a genuine cinematic statement and a practical introduction to the local industry.

"

The short film is proof of concept. What we're really commissioning is nine informed opinions about what it means to make work here.

— Program spokesperson, DocPoint & HIFF

Why Helsinki

A Cinematic City Within 10 km²

Helsinki offers an extraordinary concentration of locations within a compact, walkable city. Art Nouveau architecture, Baltic seascapes, brutalist housing blocks, boreal forests — all within a 10 km² radius.

Finland's film industry infrastructure is world-class. Experienced local crews, cutting-edge post-production facilities, and a culture of trust that makes collaboration frictionless.

The program is explicitly modelled on the city's internationally recognised 90 Day Finn initiative — its most successful effort to attract global talent since 2021.

Phase 1 — Apply

Submit Your Application

The 90 Day Film applications are a simple 3-step process:

  • Apply online between 15 February 2026 – 28 February 2026
  • Shortlist interviews in March–April 2026 – Max 10 participants will be selected
  • Final selection in May 2026

Who is this for?

Directors, producers or showrunners who have directed or produced at least one feature-length film or a series that has screened at festivals, in cinemas or on streaming platforms.

What we're looking for

  • A compelling artistic voice and distinctive visual perspective
  • A genuine interest in Helsinki and Nordic culture and expanding your company to the Nordics with long-term goals in mind

Application must include

  • Portfolio or showreel (max 10 min)
  • Free-form pitch — no fixed template
  • CV and short bio
🏆
Phase 2 — Selection & Onboarding

Selection & Onboarding

Nine participants are chosen. Selections are announced on 12 June — Helsinki Day — through a meemifiable live event. Selected participants begin remote onboarding immediately after.

Selection criteria

  • Profile and creative ambition
  • Diversity of perspective: cultural and creative
  • Potential for long-term engagement with the Finnish production ecosystem

Before the programme — Remote onboarding

  • Relocation services, including assistance with visa applications and finding an apartment
  • An online program to make the first connections and ensure a smooth arrival
  • Introduction to Helsinki's geography, culture, and seasons
  • Virtual meet-and-greet with your cohort of participants
  • A 90 Day Film onboarding box — inspired by Finland's legendary baby box — packed with useful items for your Helsinki life
🇫🇮
Phase 3 — Feel Helsinki

Feel Helsinki

Arrive in Helsinki. Walk the neighbourhoods, ride the trams, visit the archipelago. This is about immersion — finding out what resonates with you before a single frame is shot.

Immersion activities

  • Neighbourhood walks to feel the city
  • Sauna culture introduction (it's not optional, it's Finland)
  • Cultural programme — museums, galleries, live music, forest hikes

Accommodation

Furnished apartments in central Helsinki, within walking or tram distance of production facilities. Each participant receives their own private space.

💼
Phase 4 — Work Helsinki

Work Helsinki

Get set up in Helsinki's creative ecosystem and start building your local network.

What's included

  • Access to a co-working space during your stay in Helsinki
  • Discover the Finnish film landscape: make relevant connections to key industry players through networking events and business visits
  • Personal business counselling with Helsinki Partners
💡
Phase 5 — Ideation

Facilitation & Idea Development

Workshops, idea showcases, and creative facilitation sessions. Develop your concept with local industry creatives and your fellow participants.

Creative workshops

  • Concept development sessions with experienced facilitators
  • Peer feedback rounds
  • Nordic storytelling masterclasses
  • Technical workshops on Helsinki-specific production challenges (light, weather, permits)

Idea showcase

Present your refined concept to the programme team and invited industry guests. Receive structured feedback before moving into pre-production. This is collaborative, not competitive.

📍
Phase 6 — Pre-Production

Location Scouting, Casting & Crew

Scout Helsinki's extraordinary locations. Cast local talent. Match with a Helsinki-based production crew.

Production support includes

  • Dedicated local line producer assigned to your project
  • Location scouting to help identify what you're looking for
  • Casting assistance — access to local actors
  • Equipment rental and facility access

Helsinki's production advantages

Art Nouveau architecture, Baltic seascapes, islands, brutalist housing blocks, boreal forests — all within a 10 km² radius. Permits are straightforward, crews are experienced, and the city actively welcomes film production.

🎥
Phase 7 — Production

Filming in Helsinki

Shoot your 90-second to 9-minute film with full production support. Behind-the-scenes documentation captures the process for promotional and festival use.

What's provided

  • Full Helsinki-based production crew (DP, sound, gaffer, etc.)
  • Professional camera and lighting equipment
  • Behind-the-scenes documentation crew
  • On-set production management and logistics

The 90-second to 9-minute format

Deliberately compact. Short enough to complete within the residency window. Long enough to constitute a genuine cinematic statement. No genre restrictions — documentary, fiction, hybrid. Your call.

🎞️
Phase 8 — Post-Production

Post-Production

What's provided

  • Full Helsinki-based post-production services such as sound design, colour grading, music licensing, you name it
  • Cinema venue for testing and celebrating your completed result!

Taiteiden yö — Post-Production Tram

On Night of the Arts, the behind-the-scenes production process is demoed live in a dedicated tram rolling through Helsinki — an open, public showcase of how the films were made.

🎬
Phase 9 — Premiere

Attend Your Premiere & Share Your Process

Official premiere at Finnish Film Affair & Helsinki International Film Festival.

Each film screened in full, followed by the company's reflection. Industry audience: Finnish commissioners, international buyers, press, and the broader Helsinki AV ecosystem. Post-screening networking — the beginning of what comes next.

World premiere

World premiere of all 90 Days Film productions at the Finnish Film Affair, in the heart of Helsinki right on the Senate Square, each film screened in full.

Industry day

The premiere will be followed up by an industry day featuring an exhibition on the entire process, screened behind-the-scenes material, as well as an in-depth exploration of what the process meant to the filmmakers and how this production incentive model could be applied elsewhere.

🌍
Phase 10 — Show the World

Show the World

What each participant leaves with

A completed short film made in Helsinki. Direct experience of Helsinki's production incentives, locations, and talent pool. A network across the Helsinki AV ecosystem. And a story — 90 days that became a film — that is itself the best possible advertisement for doing it again, bigger.

  • International press coverage
  • Your film shared through broadcasters and festivals, using the established networks of HIFF and DocPoint
  • Your content used to showcase Helsinki around the world
  • Your short film is your proof of concept — for the feature, the series, or the documentary that comes next
  • Support for your potential expansion to Finland
